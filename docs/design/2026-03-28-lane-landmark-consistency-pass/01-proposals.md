# Proposals

## Proposal A — Landmark Grammar by Lane
- Summary: Define what visual cues each lane is allowed to own, and what it should avoid competing with.
- Why it helps: Gives placement polish a shared rule set instead of one-off map nudges.
- Cost: Low. Mostly authoring rules and a few placement adjustments.
- Risk: Needs discipline to stay small and not become a decorative expansion pass.

## Proposal B — Stronger Center Corridor Separation
- Summary: Focus only on making the planting / break lane feel distinct from plaza purchase space.
- Why it helps: Targets the most likely overlap point between pumpkin and upgrade interactions.
- Cost: Low-medium. Good for one local problem.
- Risk: Too narrow; may not help village-first or danger-lane reads.

## Proposal C — Danger Telegraph Pass
- Summary: Focus on first-contact readability for the Nolbu route using rock / lantern / sign spacing rules.
- Why it helps: Improves fairness and emotional coherence on the right side of the map.
- Cost: Low-medium.
- Risk: Valuable, but only covers one slice of the route.
