# Candidate Analysis

| Candidate | Theme Fit | Player Value | Impl Confidence | Reusability | Emotional Payoff | Risk | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|---|
| Candidate 1 | 5 | 5 | 5 | 4 | 5 | Low | Extends current quest, farming, and dialogue structure directly |
| Candidate 2 | 4 | 3 | 3 | 3 | 5 | Medium | Strong spectacle but less durable as a loop |
| Candidate 3 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 4 | 3 | Low | Good conflict, weak magical reward |

## Detailed Notes

### Candidate 1
- Strengths: preserves story order, uses existing patch flow, creates next task naturally
- Weaknesses: spectacle is moderate rather than fully cinematic
- Dependencies: quest stages, patch state, HUD attrs, one lightweight client effect channel
- MVP path: blessed seed item + special crop + reward burst + bramble sabotage
- Expansion path: full gourd-opening sequence, multiple reward types, branching retaliation outcomes

### Candidate 2
- Strengths: strongest moment-to-moment spectacle
- Weaknesses: risks becoming a one-off scene with weak follow-through
- Dependencies: more client polish, timing, validation
- MVP path: glow + reward popup
- Expansion path: camera sequences, spawned treasure props

### Candidate 3
- Strengths: simplest continuation into conflict
- Weaknesses: skips the famous reward beat players are waiting for
- Dependencies: almost none beyond dialogue and props
- MVP path: sabotage patch and clear weeds
- Expansion path: patrol aggression, extortion loops
