# Analysis

Scoring uses 1-5 where higher is better, with Risk noted separately.

| Candidate | Theme Fit | Player Value | Implementation Confidence | Reusability | Visual / Emotional Payoff | Risk | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|---|
| A. Boundary Landmark Clarification | 4 | 3 | 5 | 4 | 3 | Low | Safe and cheap, but may under-solve the actual post-slow handoff problem because it stays mostly world-side. |
| B. Recovery-to-Reentry Guidance Contract | 4 | 4 | 5 | 4 | 4 | Low | Strong for flow clarity, but can become too HUD-dependent if the space itself still feels mushy. |
| C. Retreat Lane Spatial Budget | 4 | 4 | 4 | 4 | 4 | Medium-Low | Useful for Studio tuning, but alone it does not fully author the emotional transition out of danger. |
| D. Combined Danger Re-entry Completion Pass | 5 | 5 | 4 | 5 | 5 | Medium-Low | Best completion value because the unresolved gap is not only spatial or only textual; it is the whole retreat-and-return beat. |

## Why Candidate D wins
The current project has already invested in:
- canonical verb clarity
- lane landmarks
- popup cadence hierarchy
- loop reset guidance
- danger first-contact sightlines

What still feels partially open is the **boundary event** where danger pressure ends and authored loop momentum returns.

That moment lives across both:
- world readability
- HUD/default-next-step logic

A single combined pass finishes the beat more completely than a narrower wording-only or spacing-only pass.

## Rejection notes

### Why not A only
Useful, but too narrow. A better sign or prop rhythm helps entry/exit readability, yet does not by itself decide how the player should feel once slow pressure clears.

### Why not B only
Useful, but if the retreat edge is still spatially muddy, better guidance risks papering over map ambiguity instead of finishing it.

### Why not C only
Useful, but it solves the lane more than the loop handoff. The player experience gap is ultimately about authored re-entry, not just collision-free retreat.
