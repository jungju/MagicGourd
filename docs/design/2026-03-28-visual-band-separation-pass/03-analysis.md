## Candidate A — Whole-map visual band separation contract
### Why it scores highest
- The current project already has good wording and strong event mapping.
- The recurring unresolved issue pattern is **competing read layers in one camera slice**.
- A compact whole-map contract gives every later tuning change a stable rule: do not fix overlap ad hoc.
- It improves flow clarity, interaction polish, placement consistency, and reward readability at once.

### Theme fit
This is not theme-heavy on its own, but it helps the Joseon/Heungbu world read cleanly instead of like stacked debug surfaces.

### Risk
Low, as long as it stays small and implementation-neutral.

---

## Candidate B — Plaza-only sign/popup clearance pass
### Why not chosen
The plaza is important, but recent passes already improved:
- shorter upgrade prompt reach
- distinct plaza roles
- purchase popup anchoring

A plaza-only artifact would likely repeat those decisions instead of solving the remaining cross-map layering problem.

---

## Candidate C — Farming popup headroom contract
### Why not chosen
This would improve the core loop, but much of its logic already exists in:
- feedback anchor consistency
- cadence budget
- spatial readability budget

The missing layer is broader: one rule for how all authored surfaces share the frame.

---

## Candidate D — Danger warning layering contract
### Why not chosen
Useful for fairness validation, but danger readability is already reasonably covered by the Studio validation and lane-landmark contracts.
The more valuable refinement is the general visual-band rule that also helps danger scenes.
